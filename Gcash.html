Date Release: March 2012

The Philippines consist of 7,000 islands, mountainous roads, waterways, remote villages and a population of over 90 million spread across which make it difficult for formal financial networks to reach out and provide even basic services. Around 80% of the Philippine population remains unbanked/under-banked often had to travel far to make a simple financial transactions. The use of mobile phones gained focus in the Philippines especially among the low- income groups and Globe realised that mobile phones could be used as a low cost, high penetration access channel for delivering financial services.


Thus, in 2004 Globe Telecom launched G Cash, the electronic money concept which allowed users to make purchases, pay and receive domestic payments and receive remittances by converting their actual money to electronic money and electronic money into actual money at any of the Globe’s Cash In and Cash Out Center/Outlets, via the mobile phone.


The client can register in the system through SMS inputting some key information such as name, address and phone number. The user will then receive a reference number and TAP identification number through Globe Telecom’s SMS facility. Once registered, the client can go to any Globe Cash In Center/Outlet to convert actual money to electronic money. The process addresses security concerns, as the client will provide a valid ID card and fills in the necessary forms in the Globe Cash In Center/Outlet.


Once the verification and processing is completed, the electronic money amount is sent to the mobile phone with a reference number. The client can then use the loaded electronic money to pay the rural bank by simply sending a text message with the amount and the personal identification number (PIN) to satisfy security concerns. Again, both the sender and receiver will get a confirmation of the transaction. There is bare minimum investment on equipment or infrastructure to be able to use the service since the service can be used using most ordinary mobile phones also.
